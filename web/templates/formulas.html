Core Investment Metrics
Capitalization Rate (Cap Rate)
Cap Rate = (Net Operating Income ÷ Property Value) × 100

Measures the rate of return on a real estate investment based on income generated.

Example: NOI = $50,000, Value = $500,000
Cap Rate = ($50,000 ÷ $500,000) × 100 = 10%
Cash-on-Cash Return
CoC Return = (Annual Cash Flow ÷ Initial Investment) × 100

Shows the annual return on the actual cash invested in the property.

Example: Cash Flow = $25,000, Investment = $200,000
CoC = ($25,000 ÷ $200,000) × 100 = 12.5%
Internal Rate of Return (IRR)
IRR = Rate where NPV = 0

The discount rate that makes the net present value of all cash flows equal to zero.

Complex calculation requiring financial software
Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)
DSCR = Net Operating Income ÷ Annual Debt Service

Measures the property's ability to pay its debt obligations.

Example: NOI = $60,000, Debt Service = $45,000
DSCR = $60,000 ÷ $45,000 = 1.33
Advanced Calculations

Break-Even Ratio = (Operating Expenses + Debt Service) ÷ Gross Operating Income

Shows what percentage of gross income goes toward operating expenses and debt service.

Rule of Thumb: Break-even ratio should be under 85% for investment properties.

GRM = Property Price ÷ Gross Annual Rent

Quick way to compare rental properties. Lower GRM indicates better value.

Typical Range: 8-12 for investment properties (lower is better).
