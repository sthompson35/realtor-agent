Section 1: Step-by-Step Process

1. Market ResearchDefine criteria & budget
2. Lead GenerationFind motivated sellers
3. Lead ProcessingNegotiate offers
4. Due DiligenceVerify everything
5. Close AcquisitionExecute purchase
6. Prep for ResaleMarketing package
7. Market & ListMulti-channel
8. Buyer LeadsQualify & negotiate
9. Close SaleTransfer title
10. ScaleSystematize & repeat

1. Market Research and Selection

  • Define Investment Criteria: Size, intended use, zoning classifications, value range
  • Target County Selection: Focus on lower population density counties for reduced competition
  • Analyze Market Demand: Understand buyer motivations and external factors
  • Establish Budget: Clear budget for marketing and acquisition

2. Lead Generation: Finding Motivated Sellers

  • Data Acquisition: Use DataTree or PropStream for property owner lists
  • Direct Mail: Blind offers with specific purchase prices
  • Online Marketplaces: LandWatch, Land.com, Zillow, Facebook Marketplace
  • Tax Sales: County auctions for unpaid tax parcels

3. Lead Processing and Offer Management

  • Implement CRM system for lead tracking
  • Dedicated phone line and professional buying website
  • Negotiate within predetermined price range
💡 Key Note: The initial offer via direct mail is often just a starting point for negotiation. Be prepared to revise but protect your margins.

4. Due Diligence: Critical Research

Due Diligence Checklist:
  • ☐ Verify Zoning and Use with county planning department
  • ☐ Confirm Legal Access (public road or recorded easement)
  • ☐ Assess Utility Availability (water, electric, sewer)
  • ☐ Conduct Comparable Sales Analysis
  • ☐ Title Search - liens, encumbrances, chain of title
  • ☐ Flood Zone Check via FEMA Maps
  • ☐ Wetlands Check via NWI Mapper
  • ☐ Physical characteristics via Google Earth/MapRight
  • ☐ Property visit or local photographer/drone pilot
⚠️ Critical: Lack of legal access can render a property nearly worthless. Always verify before closing!

5. Closing the Acquisition

  • Use title company/attorney for higher-value transactions
  • Execute Purchase Agreement and prepare Special Warranty Deed
  • Use third-party escrow for fund transfer
  • Record deed with county recorder's office

6-9. Resale Preparation through Disposition

  • Gather documentation: recorded deed, property maps, reports
  • Obtain professional photos and drone imagery
  • List on: LandWatch, Land.com, LandFlip, Facebook Marketplace, MLS
  • Contact adjacent property owners
  • Offer both cash price and seller financing options

10. Systematize and Scale

  • Track leads, conversion rates, marketing costs, profit per deal
  • Create templates for checklists, agreements, descriptions
  • Automate direct mail, consider virtual assistants

Section 5: Key Formulas and Calculations

Maximum Allowable Offer (MAO)

MAO = (Estimated Resale Value × 0.40) − Known Costs

Example: 5 acres at $4,500/acre median, $800 back taxes, $1,500 closing costs:

  • Resale Value: 5 × $4,500 = $22,500
  • Apply 40%: $22,500 × 0.40 = $9,000
  • Subtract Costs: $9,000 − $800 − $1,500 = $6,700 MAO

Return on Investment (ROI)

ROI (%) = (Net Profit ÷ Total Investment) × 100

Example: Buy $5,000, costs $1,500, sell $15,000:

  • Total Investment: $6,500
  • Net Profit: $15,000 − $6,500 = $8,500
  • ROI: ($8,500 ÷ $6,500) × 100 = 130.8%

Profit Margin

Profit Margin (%) = (Net Profit ÷ Selling Price) × 100

Using above: ($8,500 ÷ $15,000) × 100 = 56.7%
