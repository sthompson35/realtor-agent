Acquisition Tips
Always Have Multiple Offers Ready

Never make just one offer. Prepare 3-5 different offer structures with varying terms, contingencies, and closing dates. This gives you negotiation leverage and shows sellers you're serious.

Get Seller Motivation First

Before discussing price, understand why the seller is selling. Their motivation (divorce, relocation, financial distress) will determine how much flexibility they have on price and terms.

Use Contingencies Strategically

Don't remove contingencies just to make your offer more attractive. Use them to protect yourself during due diligence. The right contingencies can actually strengthen your offer.

Focus on Terms Over Price

Sometimes better terms (seller financing, flexible closing, included appliances) can be more valuable than a slightly lower price. Calculate the actual value of each term.

Due Diligence Tips
Hire Your Own Inspector

Never rely on seller-provided inspection reports. Hire your own certified inspector who works for you, not the seller. Be present during the inspection.

Check All Utilities

Verify all utility accounts are in the seller's name and current. Check for any outstanding balances or disputed charges that could transfer to you.

Research the Neighborhood

Drive the neighborhood at different times. Check for nuisances, school quality, crime rates, and future development plans that could affect property values.

Management & Operations Tips
Screen Tenants Thoroughly

Use comprehensive tenant screening: credit check, criminal background, employment verification, and references. A good tenant is worth their weight in gold.

Keep Reserves for Repairs

Always maintain 6-12 months of reserves for unexpected repairs and vacancies. Properties always need maintenance, and tenants always move eventually.

Document Everything

Keep detailed records of all communications, repairs, and transactions. Good documentation protects you legally and helps with tax deductions.
